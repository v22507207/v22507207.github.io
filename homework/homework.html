FBI searches for motive in assassination attempt on former presodent

Republican presidential candidate Donald Trump is assisted by Secret Service personnel after gunfire rang out during a campaign rally Saturday in Butler, Pa. PHOTOS BY BRENDAN

Dan Morrison, Elizabeth Weise and N'dea Yancey-Bragg USA TODAY

BETHEL PARK, Pa. - The nation reeled Sunday from the shocking news that a 20-year-old gunman had attempted to assassinate former President Donald Trump at a political rally in Pennsylvania on Saturday evening, a brazen act of political violence the likes of which America has not seen since an attempt on President Ronald Reagan's life 43 years ago. • Trump, the presumptive Republican presidential nominee, was recovering after he was injured in the right ear when a sniper opened fire with an AR-15-style rifle from a rooftop about 400 feet outside a campaign rally in Butler, Pennsylvania. One spectator was killed and two others were “critically injured,” the Secret Service said.
